Commercial Sale-Room

South of Duckworth St., East of McBride's hill


Original Text

The image to the right is a snapshot of an advertisement placed by W.H. Mare, auctioneer, for an auction taking place at the Commercial Sale-Room, and ran in the Morning Chronicle of date, 1865.

Location

The Commercial Sale Room was part of a building complex known as the Commercial Buildings. The approximate location of this complex was on the South side of Duckworth Street, East of McBride's Hill Rd. This complex is no longer extant.

The commercial buildings seem to have been a place not specific to any one business or merchant, but rather, a public space which could be used to host commercial events such as public auctions or large sales of merchandise.


Research

W. H. Mare

William Henry Mare, Esquire, was a broker, commission merchant, and notary public. His business headquarters and personal residence appear to have been the same place, 183 Water Street.

In the year 1865 (and possibly other years), Mare was the Treasurer of the Newfoundland Church Society.
